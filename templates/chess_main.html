Chess Pieces

Pawn

The pawn is the weakest piece. It can move only one space forward and attacks diagonally to the front.

Knight

The knight is a minor piece. It is a beginning and middlegame piece. It has the unique ability to jump over pieces. The knight moves in an 'L' shape. Two spaces forward, backward, left, or right and one space to the left and right.

Bishop

The Bishop is a minor piece. It is useful for the middle game. It can move only diagonally for as many spaces as possible.

Rook

The rook is a strong endgame piece. It normally remains inactive until many of the other pieces are removed from the board. The rook can move forward, backward, left, and right as many spaces as possible.

Queen

The queen is a very powerful piece. For this reason, it is important to keep your queen safe. The queen can move in all directions for as many spaces as possible.

King

The king is the most important piece. If the king is taken then the player loses the game. The king can move one space in each direction.

Chess Openings

In the game of chess, the white pieces move first and then black moves a piece in response to white. The first move by white directs the game and there are only a few moves that help white gain a starting advantage. Having control of the central squares is necessary for this advantage. The two first moves immediately give white the first claim to central squares.
